THE VULTURES

Part I

Vultures are surrounded by more traditions and legends than any other nocturnal creature. Since time immemorial, they have been regarded as monstrous flesh eaters, charismatic masters and terrible masters of the undead. The Vulture race, the most powerful of the undead, was born of an unholy ritual and a dark elixir many thousands of years ago.


Though the Vultures are physically strong, fast, and hardy, it is not these qualities that make them so intimidating. It is the will and personal power that makes the Vulture crave great power, and it is this that drives the power that almost all living creatures lack.


Vultures are endowed with supernatural ability to control the dead, to become the natural leaders of the armies of the undead that ravage the world. Although hated and persecuted, vultures also lurk in human society. In human society, they take the guise of aristocrats or live in abandoned citadels at the back of civilization.
Part II

The inner power of the Vultures over death is so strong that you can never know for sure if they are dead or not. They always come back and inflict terrible vengeance on their victims when they don't expect it at all. The Vultures may sleep for years, decades, even centuries, while their servants prepare the opportunity to restore them to their former power.


Once the Vultures are filled with power again, they will assemble a horde of the undead and go to war, fighting to fulfill their vicious ambitions. The Vultures gather secret communities in the ruins of ruined palaces and citadels. They know that no one will disturb them. They know that they are the masters of this world.
Part III

A relentless horde, the unstoppable and terrifying undead are some of the most dangerous foes of all mythical creatures. The resurrected Vultures are a terrifying and mighty army. You cannot disagree with them, they cannot be bribed or stopped. They know neither fear nor pity. It needs neither sleep, nor warmth, nor drink, nor good food.


Nothing strikes fear into the hearts of men like the sight of the reviving dead. Their corpses are wrapped in burial robes, and they are armed with ancient and rusty weapons - visions of doom. Faced with that which haunts human beings in nightmares, most mortals feel indescribable horror. And only the bravest warriors will remain in their positions...
